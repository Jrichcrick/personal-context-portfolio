NYC Course Notes

  • Mile 1 — Verrazano Bridge: Steep uphill. Run easy. Let people go. Congestion forces this anyway.
  • Miles 2–13 — Brooklyn: Flat and fast. Lock into 9:00/mi and ignore the crowd energy — this is where people go out too hard.
  • Miles 15–16 — Queensboro Bridge: Long uphill, no crowd noise inside. Stay steady. Don't panic.
  • Mile 16 — 1st Avenue: Crowd erupts. Do NOT surge. Most common place people blow up.
  • Miles 20–23 — Bronx & Harlem: Legs are heavy. Shorten stride, stay tall, focus on form.
  • Miles 23–26.2 — Central Park: Rolling hills. Cat Hill at ~mile 24.5. Spend whatever's left after mile 25.
If it's warm (above 60°F): add 20–30 sec/mile. A smart early slow-down saves your finish.

Pre-Run Nutrition

What works for you specifically
  • Lower fiber before runs — you feel heavier and sluggish when fiber is high
  • More protein/meat the day before — you feel better and run better
  • This is your gut telling you something real. Honor it every time.

Day Before a Long Run or Quality Session

MealEat thisAvoid this
BreakfastEggs, bacon or sausage, white toastOatmeal, bran, raw vegetables
LunchChicken/beef + white rice or pastaLarge salads, legumes, whole grains
DinnerSteak or chicken + white pasta or riceBroccoli, beans, lentils, heavy sauces
SnacksBanana, white bread + peanut butterHigh-fiber bars, raw fruit with skin
Hydrate more than usual — aim for pale yellow urine by end of day.

Race Morning (same rules for long runs)

  • 2–3 hours before: 400–600 cal. Bagel + peanut butter + banana. Don't experiment.
  • 60 min before: Small snack if needed (half banana, a few crackers)
  • Coffee: Fine if you normally drink it. Don't add it race day if you usually don't.
  • No new foods race morning. Ever.

Fueling During Runs

When to fuel

  • Under 60 min: Water only
  • 60–90 min: Optional gel at the end
  • Over 90 min: Fuel consistently — don't wait until empty

Race day schedule

  • Gel at miles 5, 10, 15, 20
  • Water at every aid station (small sip)
  • Gatorade every other station
  • Only use what you trained with

Long Run Fueling Protocol

  • First gel at mile 5 (roughly 45–50 min in)
  • Then every 40–45 minutes after that
  • Wash down with water, not Gatorade (prevents GI issues)
  • Electrolytes every 45–60 min in warm weather

Gel Options — pick one, stick with it all training

  • Gu Energy — most common, easy to find everywhere
  • Maurten — gentler on stomach, worth trying if you've had GI issues
  • Spring Energy — real food, works well for some
NYC provides Gatorade Endurance on course — train with it, or carry your own.

Post-Run Recovery

Within 30–45 minutes of finishing

This window matters most for recovery, especially now. Don't skip it because you're not hungry.

  • Target: 20–30g protein + 40–60g carbs
  • Chocolate milk (genuinely ideal)
  • Greek yogurt + banana
  • Eggs + toast
  • Protein shake + fruit
Each pound lost during a run = ~16oz to replenish. Drink consistently for 4–6 hours after long runs.

Sleep

Sleep is when your body actually builds the fitness you earned in training. Skimping doesn't just make you tired — it literally reduces the adaptation. For older athletes this effect is stronger.

Targets

  • Regular nights: 7.5–8 hours minimum
  • Before long runs: 8–9 hours
  • Peak weeks 9–16: prioritize over everything non-essential

Practical tips

  • Consistent wake time anchors your rhythm more than bedtime
  • No alcohol within 3 hours of sleep
  • No hard training within 4 hours of bed
  • Cool room: 65–68°F
  • Dark room — blackout shades or sleep mask
Race week: don't stress about the night before the race — almost everyone sleeps badly. Friday Oct 31 (two nights before) is the one that matters. Protect that one.

Stretching

Before runs — Dynamic (5 min)

Never static stretch cold muscles.

  • Leg swings front-back + side-to-side: 10 each leg
  • Hip circles: 10 each direction
  • High knees: 20 reps
  • Butt kicks: 20 reps
  • Lateral shuffles: 10 yards each way
  • First mile always slower than easy pace

After runs — Static (10 min)

Hold each 30–45 sec. Don't rush.

  • Standing quad pull
  • Figure-4 (glutes, piriformis)
  • Runner's lunge (hip flexors)
  • Seated hamstring reach
  • Calf stretch against wall
  • IT band cross-stretch (standing)

After long runs — add foam rolling (10–15 min)

  • IT band — slow, painful, necessary
  • Quads — front and sides
  • Calves — especially around Achilles insertion
  • Upper back / thoracic spine

Managing Hard Days

Flip to easy if...

  • Legs still heavy from last session
  • Slept poorly 2+ nights in a row
  • Resting HR elevated 5+ bpm
  • You feel "off" but can't explain it

Take a rest day if...

  • Sharp or one-sided pain (knee, shin, foot)
  • Any heel or Achilles pain in the morning
  • Fever or illness
  • Extreme fatigue after normal sleep
When in doubt, go easy. Missing one quality session hurts less than getting injured.

Race Week Nutrition

  • No new foods all week. Not the expo restaurant, not the pasta dinner the night before.
  • Carb load starting Wed Oct 28: 70–75% of calories from carbs, cut fat and fiber
  • Salt more than usual the last 2–3 days — helps water retention, prevents hyponatremia
  • No alcohol after Wednesday Oct 28
  • Friday Oct 30: Eat early. Sleep early. This is the important night.
  • Race morning: Eat 2.5–3 hours before your corral time. NYC starts early — work backwards from your gun time.
