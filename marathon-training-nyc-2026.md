## Training Paces

Based on your actual running (9:20 easy days, 8:30 on harder shorter runs).

| Zone | Current | By Race Day | Feel |
|------|---------|-------------|------|
| Easy (E) | 9:20–9:45/mi | 9:00–9:20/mi | Fully conversational |
| Marathon Pace (MP) | — | **9:00/mi** | Comfortably hard, sustained |
| Tempo (T) | 8:20–8:30/mi | 8:00–8:10/mi | Hard but controlled, 20–40 min max |
| Intervals (I) | 7:50–8:00/mi | 7:30–7:45/mi | 5K–10K effort, short repeats |

**One thing to know:** your easy pace (9:20) and your marathon goal pace (9:00) are very close. That gap will widen as your fitness improves and your easy pace gets faster — by race day your easy runs will feel like a different gear than 9:00 race pace. On recovery days and the day after hard sessions, slow down to 9:45 or beyond if your legs need it. There's no floor on easy.

## Weekly Schedule

### Your week, every week

| Day | Role |
|-----|------|
| Monday | Squats + boot camp — this is your strength & cross-train day |
| Tuesday | Easy shakeout — short, loose, legs only (3–4 mi) |
| Wednesday | Quality run — the only hard running day all week |
| Thursday | Easy run |
| Friday | **Rest** (weeks 1–6) → **Medium-long run** (weeks 7+) |
| Saturday | Rest |
| Sunday | Long run |

### Why this works for you
- Monday squats are hard on legs — Tuesday shakeout is intentionally short to flush them out
- Wednesday is your only hard run — it sits between easy Tuesday and easy Thursday
- Friday adds as the 5th day at week 7 when your base supports it; rest Saturday protects Sunday's long run
- No back-to-back hard efforts anywhere in the plan

### Weeks 1–6: 4 running days (Tue/Wed/Thu/Sun)
### Weeks 7–21: 5 running days (Tue/Wed/Thu/Fri/Sun)

## NYC Marathon Course Notes

- **Mile 1 — Verrazano Bridge:** Steep uphill start. Run easy and let people go. The congestion forces this anyway.
- **Miles 2–13 — Brooklyn:** Flat and fast. Enormous crowds. Lock into 9:00/mi and ignore the energy — this is where people go out too fast.
- **Miles 15–16 — Queensboro Bridge:** Long uphill, no crowd noise inside the bridge. Stay steady. Don't panic.
- **Mile 16 — 1st Avenue:** The crowd erupts. Do NOT surge. This is the most common place people blow up in the whole race.
- **Miles 20–23 — Bronx & Harlem:** This is where it gets hard. Shorten stride, stay tall, focus on form.
- **Miles 23–26.2 — Central Park:** Rolling hills. Cat Hill at ~mile 24.5 is the last real test. Anything left in the tank, spend it after mile 25.

**If it's warm (above 60°F):** Add 20–30 sec/mile to your target pace. A smart slow-down early saves your finish.

## Nutrition & Fueling

- **Runs under 75 min:** Water only
- **Long runs:** Gel every 40–45 min, starting at mile 5. Practice this — your gut needs to learn it.
- **Race day:** Gel at miles 5, 10, 15, 20. Stick with what you trained with.
- **Day before long runs/quality sessions:** Lower fiber, more protein/meat. This is your pattern — follow it.
- **Carb loading:** Start 3 days before race. High carb, low fiber, low fat.
- **Race morning:** 400–600 cal, 2–3 hours before gun (bagel + banana + peanut butter).

---

## Injury Prevention

- **Monday boot camp already covers your strength work** — glutes, legs, core. That's the plan. Don't add a separate strength session on top of it during hard weeks.
- **Never increase weekly mileage more than 10%** — recovery weeks built in every 4th week.
- **Foam roll after every long run** — quads, IT band, calves.
- **8+ hours of sleep** during peak weeks (9–16).
- **If something hurts two runs in a row**, take an extra rest day. Don't push through sharp or one-sided pain.
