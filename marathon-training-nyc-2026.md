# NYC Marathon 2026 — 21-Week Training Plan

**Race date:** Sunday, November 1, 2026  
**Start date:** Thursday, June 4, 2026  
**Distance:** 26.2 miles  
**Course:** All 5 boroughs — Verrazano Bridge → Brooklyn → Queens → Manhattan → Bronx → Central Park finish

---

## Pace Guide

Set your paces based on a recent 5K or half marathon using a race predictor, then slot them here:

| Zone | Description | Feel |
|------|-------------|------|
| Easy (E) | Fully conversational | Can say full sentences |
| Marathon Pace (MP) | Goal race pace | Comfortably hard |
| Tempo (T) | ~25 sec/mile faster than MP | Hard but controlled, 20–40 min max |
| Interval (I) | 5K–10K effort | 400–1200m repeats with recovery |
| Long Run | Mostly easy, finish miles at MP | |

## NYC Marathon Course Notes

The course has character — train accordingly:

- **Mile 1:** Sharp uphill over the Verrazano Bridge. Do not go out fast. Let people pass you.
- **Miles 2–13 (Brooklyn):** Mostly flat and fast. Crowds are enormous. Stay disciplined on pace.
- **Mile 15–16 (Queensboro Bridge):** Significant uphill with no crowd noise inside the bridge. Stay steady.
- **Mile 16 (1st Ave):** The crowd explodes. Do NOT surge here — this is where many people blow up.
- **Miles 20–23 (Bronx & Harlem):** Legs are tired. Shorten stride, stay tall.
- **Miles 23–26.2 (Central Park):** Rolling hills. Cat Hill (~mile 24.5) is real. Bank energy to push here.

**Heat strategy:** NYC in November averages 40–55°F but can vary. If it's warm on race day, back off MP by 10–20 sec/mile.

## Nutrition & Fueling

- **Training runs under 75 min:** Water only, no gels needed
- **Long runs:** Practice fueling strategy — gel every 45 min, start at mile 5
- **Race day target:** Gel at miles 5, 10, 15, 20 (or per your training)
- **Carb loading:** 3 days out, increase carbs, reduce fiber and fat
- **Race morning:** 2–3 hrs before gun, 400–600 cal (bagel, banana, oatmeal)
- **Hydration:** Drink to thirst, not on schedule. Practice with Gatorade on long runs to avoid GI issues on race day.

---

## Injury Prevention

- **10% rule:** Never increase weekly mileage more than 10% week-over-week (recovery weeks built in)
- **Strength work:** 2× per week — glute bridges, single-leg squats, calf raises, hip flexor work
- **Foam rolling:** Quads, IT band, calves — especially after long runs
- **Sleep:** 8+ hours during peak weeks (weeks 9–16)
- **Warning signs:** Shin pain, IT band tightness, Achilles soreness — address immediately, don't push through
