## Schedule Structure

### Weeks 1–6: 4 days/week

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Rest | Quality | Rest | Easy | Rest | Medium | Long |

Hard Tuesday → rest Wednesday → easy Thursday → rest Friday prevents any back-to-back hard efforts. Saturday medium is easy enough that Sunday's long run is never compromised.

### Weeks 7–21: 5 days/week

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Rest | Quality | Easy | Easy | Rest | Medium | Long |

Wednesday easy run is added as the 5th day. Tuesday is still the only hard day mid-week — Wednesday and Thursday are both easy recovery miles.

## NYC Marathon Course Notes

- **Mile 1 — Verrazano Bridge:** Steep uphill start. Run easy and let people go. The congestion forces this anyway.
- **Miles 2–13 — Brooklyn:** Flat and fast. Enormous crowds. Lock into 9:00/mi and ignore the energy — this is where people go out too fast.
- **Miles 15–16 — Queensboro Bridge:** Long uphill, no crowd noise inside the bridge. Stay steady. Don't panic.
- **Mile 16 — 1st Avenue:** The crowd erupts. Do NOT surge. This is the most common place people blow up in the whole race.
- **Miles 20–23 — Bronx & Harlem:** This is where it gets hard. Shorten stride, stay tall, focus on form.
- **Miles 23–26.2 — Central Park:** Rolling hills. Cat Hill at ~mile 24.5 is the last real test. Anything left in the tank, spend it after mile 25.

**If it's warm (above 60°F):** Add 20–30 sec/mile to your target pace. A smart slow-down early saves your finish.

## Nutrition & Fueling

- **Runs under 75 min:** Water only
- **Long runs:** Gel every 40–45 min, starting at mile 5. Practice this — your gut needs to learn it.
- **Race day:** Gel at miles 5, 10, 15, 20. Stick with whatever you practiced in training.
- **Carb loading:** Start 3 days before race. High carb, low fiber, low fat.
- **Race morning:** 400–600 cal, 2–3 hours before gun (bagel + banana + peanut butter is reliable).
- **Hydration:** Drink Gatorade on long runs so race day electrolytes aren't a surprise to your stomach.

---

## Injury Prevention

- **Strength 2×/week on rest days** — glute bridges, single-leg squats, calf raises, hip flexor stretches. 20 minutes is enough.
- **Never increase weekly mileage more than 10%** — recovery weeks are built in every 4th week for this reason.
- **Foam roll after every long run** — quads, IT band, calves.
- **8+ hours of sleep** during peak weeks (9–16). This is when your body actually adapts.
- **If something hurts two runs in a row**, take an extra rest day and reassess. Don't push through sharp or one-sided pain.
